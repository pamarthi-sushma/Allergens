    CONTACT DERMATITIS

    To alleviate contact dermatitis, start by identifying and avoiding the irritant or allergen triggering the reaction.
    Applying cold compresses, using over-the-counter or prescription topical corticosteroids, and taking oatmeal baths can help soothe inflammation and itching.
    Regular use of fragrance-free moisturizers, oral antihistamines, and calamine lotion can further provide relief. It's essential to refrain from scratching to prevent worsening the condition
    and potential infection. If symptoms persist, seeking advice from a healthcare professional is crucial for accurate diagnosis and appropriate treatment, which may include prescription medications or additional medical
    interventions for severe cases.

    ANGIOEDEMA

    Antihistamines, available over the counter, may help counteract allergic reactions contributing to angioedema.
    Avoiding known triggers, such as specific foods or medications, is essential. In addition, staying hydrated can promote overall skin health. However, if angioedema persists or worsens,
    it's crucial to seek medical attention promptly, as severe cases may require prescription medications or further evaluation by a healthcare professional to address the underlying cause.
