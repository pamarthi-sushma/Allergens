    CHICKEN POX

    Chickenpox is a highly contagious viral infection caused by the varicella-zoster virus.
    It typically manifests with an itchy rash, fever, and flu-like symptoms. The rash starts as red spots that evolve into
    fluid-filled blisters before forming scabs. It's important to seek medical advice for proper management, especially in high-risk
    groups like pregnant women or individuals with weakened immune systems. Vaccination is an effective preventive measure
    against chickenpox.

    MEASLES

    Measles is a highly contagious viral infection caused by the measles virus. It manifests with symptoms like high fever,
    cough, runny nose, and a distinctive red rash. The rash starts on the face and spreads throughout the body. Complications can be
    severe, including pneumonia and encephalitis. Vaccination is a key preventive measure, providing immunity and reducing the risk of
    outbreaks. Early diagnosis and medical care are crucial to manage symptoms and prevent the spread of the virus.
